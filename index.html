Matthew Newton

I recently graduated with a DPhil from the University of Oxford. My thesis was titled “Analysis of Robust Neural Networks for Control” and investigated the robustness and safety properties of Neural Networks when used in control feedback systems. From this research I have gained knowledge in Neural Networks, Optimisation, Control Theory and Reinforcement Learning. Throughout my DPhil I presented at numerous international conferences and undertook several teaching roles, which has given me the confidence to communicate complex scientific concepts to relevant audiences.

I have also undertaken internships in Quantitative Finance and am interested in how the latest machine learning algorithms can be applied in all industries and how these algorithms can be implemented safely and responsibly. I am a highly motivated individual, who is looking to find his next career challenge. If you would like to contact me about any opportunities or projects, then please do reach out.

Outside of my career, I love skiing, surfing and hiking. I recently completed a season working as a Ski Instructor in Canada, which has been a life-long ambition of mine. I previously competed at a national level for my University’s Athletics Club in the 400m and was elected as Men’s Captain.
